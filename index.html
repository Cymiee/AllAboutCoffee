Beans & Origins

Most coffee comes from two species. Where it’s grown + how it’s processed shapes flavor.

Arabica

Sweet + complex

Often fruity, floral, chocolatey. Typically higher acidity and aroma.

  • Common at higher altitudes
  • More delicate plant
  • Often “specialty coffee” focused
Robusta

Stronger + more caffeine

More bitter, heavier body, great crema—often used in espresso blends.

  • Hardier plant
  • Higher caffeine
  • Nutty / woody / dark chocolate notes
Processing

How the fruit is removed changes taste

  • Washed: clean, crisp, bright
  • Natural: fruit-forward, sweet, sometimes funky
  • Honey: syrupy sweetness + clarity

Classic Coffee Drinks

Most café drinks are espresso + water + milk (and sometimes foam/chocolate). Ratios vary by café.

Espresso

Concentrated shot with crema. Strong, small, intense.

Americano

Espresso + hot water. Similar strength to filter coffee, different texture.

Latte

Espresso + lots of steamed milk + thin foam cap.

Cappuccino

Espresso + milk + more foam (airier feel).

Flat White

Espresso-forward milk drink with very thin microfoam.

Mocha

Latte + chocolate. Dessert-y and rich.

Brewing Basics

Great coffee is mostly: grind size, ratio, water temp, and time. Change one variable at a time.

Ratios (starting points)

  • Filter: 1:15 to 1:17 (coffee:water)
  • French press: 1:12 to 1:15

Water temperature

  • Most brews: ~92–96°C
  • Darker roasts: slightly cooler can reduce bitterness

Methods (what they emphasize)

  • Pour-over: clarity, bright notes
  • French press: heavier body, more oils
  • AeroPress: flexible, can go strong or clean
  • Espresso: intense, textured, concentrated

Glossary

Quick words you’ll see on bags and menus.

Extraction

How much flavor you pull out of the grounds. Sour = under, bitter = over.

Body

Mouthfeel. Light/tea-like vs heavy/syrupy.

Crema

The foamy top on espresso—emulsified oils + gases.

Bloom

In pour-over, the first pour that releases CO₂ (helps even extraction).
