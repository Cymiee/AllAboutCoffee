Beans & Origins

Most coffee comes from two species. Where it’s grown + how it’s processed shapes flavor.

Arabica

Sweet + complex

Often fruity, floral, chocolatey. Typically higher acidity and aroma.

  • Common at higher altitudes
  • More delicate plant
  • Often “specialty coffee” focused
Robusta

Stronger + more caffeine

More bitter, heavier body, great crema—often used in espresso blends.

  • Hardier plant
  • Higher caffeine
  • Nutty / woody / dark chocolate notes
Processing

How the fruit is removed changes taste

  • Washed: clean, crisp, bright
  • Natural: fruit-forward, sweet, sometimes funky
  • Honey: syrupy sweetness + clarity

Brewing Methods

Six ways to brew — how each works, what it tastes like, and how hard it is.

Easy

Instant Coffee

Mild, convenient, and always consistent — the simplest cup you can make.

  • No equipment needed
  • Ready in under two minutes
  • Consistent result every time
  1. Boil water and let it cool for 30 seconds.
  2. Add 1–2 teaspoons of granules to your cup.
  3. Pour in hot water and stir until dissolved.
  4. Adjust granule amount to taste.
Easy

French Press

Full-bodied, rich, and oily — the method that lets the coffee's natural oils shine.

  • Cheap kit, no paper filters
  • Extracts oils for a heavier body
  • Easy to scale up
  1. Coarsely grind your coffee.
  2. Add grounds and pour 92 °C water over them.
  3. Stir gently to saturate all the grounds.
  4. Steep for 4 minutes with the lid on.
  5. Press the plunger down slowly and evenly.
  6. Pour immediately to stop extraction.
Easy

Drip / Filter Machine

Clean, balanced, and approachable — great for brewing multiple cups at once.

  • Completely hands-free once set up
  • Highly repeatable results
  • Ideal for large batches
  1. Place a paper filter in the basket.
  2. Add medium-ground coffee (about 1 tbsp per 180 ml).
  3. Fill the water reservoir to your desired level.
  4. Press brew and let the machine do the work.
  5. Serve once the cycle is complete.
Intermediate

Pour Over

Clean, bright, and nuanced — the method that lets origin flavours speak loudest.

  • Highlights subtle origin flavours
  • Precise control over every variable
  • Rewarding, meditative process
  1. Rinse the paper filter with hot water, then discard the rinse water.
  2. Add medium-fine ground coffee to the filter.
  3. Pour twice the coffee weight in water to bloom — wait 30 seconds.
  4. Continue pouring in slow, steady circles from centre outward.
  5. Total brew time should be around 3 minutes.
Intermediate

AeroPress

Smooth, low-acid, and flexible — one of the most forgiving brewers you can own.

  • Fast brew time under 2 minutes
  • Compact and travel-friendly
  • Works with a wide range of grind sizes
  1. Insert a filter and rinse it, then lock the cap on.
  2. Add fine-medium ground coffee to the chamber.
  3. Pour 85–92 °C water and stir for 10 seconds.
  4. Steep for 1–2 minutes.
  5. Press the plunger down slowly over 30 seconds.
Intermediate

Moka Pot

Strong, bold, and espresso-like — rich stovetop coffee with no electricity required.

  • No electricity needed
  • Produces a rich, concentrated brew
  • Develops a natural crema
  1. Fill the bottom chamber with water up to the safety valve.
  2. Add finely ground coffee to the basket — level but don't tamp.
  3. Screw the top and bottom chambers together firmly.
  4. Place on medium heat and leave the lid open.
  5. Remove from heat when you hear a gurgling sound.

Glossary

Quick words you’ll see on bags and menus.

Extraction

How much flavor you pull out of the grounds. Sour = under, bitter = over.

Body

Mouthfeel. Light/tea-like vs heavy/syrupy.

Crema

The foamy top on espresso—emulsified oils + gases.

Bloom

In pour-over, the first pour that releases CO₂ (helps even extraction).
