Beans & Origins

Most coffee you drink comes from two species: Arabica and Robusta. Where and how a coffee is grown and processed shapes its flavor.

Arabica

Usually sweeter and more complex. Often notes like fruit, chocolate, florals.

  • Typically higher acidity and aroma
  • Grown at higher altitudes
  • More sensitive plant → harder to grow

Robusta

Stronger, more bitter, and higher caffeine. Often used in espresso blends.

  • Higher caffeine, thicker crema
  • Hardier plant → easier to grow
  • Flavor can be nutty, woody, or dark chocolate

Processing methods

Processing is how the fruit is removed from the seed (the “bean”). It changes sweetness, clarity, and fruitiness.

  • Washed: clean, crisp flavors; brighter acidity.
  • Natural: fruit-forward, sweeter; can be funky.
  • Honey: in-between; syrupy sweetness + clarity.
Tip: origin + process + roast tells you a lot before you even brew.
