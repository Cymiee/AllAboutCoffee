Brewing Basics

Great coffee is mostly about grind size, ratio, water temperature, and brew time. If it tastes sour → under-extracted. Bitter → over-extracted.

Ratios

  • Filter coffee: 1:15 to 1:17 (coffee:water)
  • French press: a little stronger, often 1:12 to 1:15

Water temperature

  • Most brews: ~92–96°C (not boiling)
  • Darker roasts: slightly cooler can reduce bitterness

Methods (what they emphasize)

  • V60 / Pour-over: clarity, bright notes
  • French press: heavier body, more oils
  • AeroPress: flexible; can mimic espresso-like strength
  • Espresso: intense, concentrated, texture-heavy
Rule of thumb: change one variable at a time when dialing in.
